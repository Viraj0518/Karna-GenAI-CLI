# Cognitive Testing Report: Vaccine Intent & Trust Questionnaire

## Critical Findings

### Comprehension Risk
- **Item 7:** "The CDC has my best interests in mind" is abstract and assumes shared understanding of institutional motivation. Respondents may conflate "interests" with "competence" or "benevolence."
- **Item 10:** Double-negative construction ("hasn't changed" vs. "changed too much") requires cognitive effort; vulnerable to misinterpretation.

### Double-Barrel Wording
- **Item 3:** Conflates two distinct constructs—personal protection AND family protection. Respondent who agrees they're protected but doubts family coverage is forced to choose.
- **Item 8:** Combines two attributes ("clearly" and "honestly"). Respondents may trust honesty but doubt clarity, or vice versa.
- **Item 13:** Asks about both "effectiveness" and "safety" in a single item; these are orthogonal constructs.

### Socially-Desirable Bias Risk
- **Item 4:** Recommendation to friends/family is subject to approval-seeking bias; respondents may inflate agreement to appear prosocial.
- **Item 6:** Trust in institutions is high-stakes politically; respondents may bias responses toward social acceptability.
- **Item 9:** Framing "scientific evidence" as objectively superior to "anecdotes" creates implicit moral hierarchy; may elicit acquiescence from respondents wanting to appear rational.

### Acquiescence-Bias Risk
- **Item 2:** Simple agreement statement with no negative counterpart in immediate context; yes-saying may dominate.
- **Item 14:** Influence of social media is culturally salient and often criticized; respondents may over-report influence to align with prevailing narrative.

### Scale Construction Issue
- **Item 5:** Multiple-choice format differs from Likert scale; mixes categorical and ordinal measurement approaches, complicating analysis.
- **Item 11:** Single-selection item lacks ordinal structure; cannot be combined with 5-point Likert items in aggregate scoring.

## Revision Recommendations

### 1. **Disaggregate Double-Barrel Items**
   - **Item 3** → Split into two items: (a) "Vaccination is the best way to protect *me* from COVID-19" and (b) "Vaccination is the best way to protect *my family* from COVID-19."
   - **Item 8** → Split into two items: (a) "The CDC provides clear vaccine safety information" and (b) "The CDC provides honest vaccine safety information."
   - **Item 13** → Split into two items: (a) "I feel well-informed about vaccine effectiveness" and (b) "I feel well-informed about vaccine safety."

### 2. **Add Negatively-Worded Items & Clarify Abstract Language**
   - **Item 7** → Replace "best interests in mind" with concrete behaviors: "The CDC bases vaccine recommendations on scientific evidence" (and add inverse: "The CDC prioritizes politics over safety in vaccine decisions").
   - **Item 10** → Simplify to: "The CDC's vaccine guidance has been inconsistent" (removes double-negative).
   - Add Item 15: "I would NOT recommend the COVID-19 vaccine to close contacts" (inverse of Item 4 to reduce acquiescence bias).

### 3. **Standardize Response Formats & Reduce Social Desirability Cues**
   - Convert **Item 5** from checkbox to single-select categorical (align with Item 11 structure).
   - Reframe **Item 9** neutrally: "I rely on scientific studies when making vaccine decisions" (remove value judgment of "more than" anecdotes).
   - For **Item 14**, add temporal specificity: "In the past 3 months, social media has influenced my views about vaccines" (reduces telescoping and perceived norm-following).
