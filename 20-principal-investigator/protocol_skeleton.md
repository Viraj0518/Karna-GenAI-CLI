# Protocol: Maternal Health Communications in Low-SES Urban Populations
## A 30-Focus-Group Qualitative Study

### Background
Despite advances in maternal healthcare, low-socioeconomic-status (SES) urban populations experience persistent disparities in prenatal care engagement and maternal health outcomes. Communication barriers—including health literacy gaps, cultural misalignment, and limited access to trusted information sources—contribute to these inequities. This study examines how women in low-SES urban contexts interpret, engage with, and share maternal health communications, informing evidence-based interventions to reduce maternal mortality and morbidity in this vulnerable population.

### Aims
**Aim 1:** Identify communication channels, messages, and trusted messengers that resonate with pregnant and postpartum women in low-SES urban communities.

**Aim 2:** Explore barriers and facilitators to maternal health information-seeking, retention, and behavioral action among this population.

**Aim 3:** Develop culturally-tailored communication recommendations for public health agencies and healthcare systems to improve maternal health outcomes.

### Design
Mixed-purposive qualitative design using focus groups (6–8 participants each) stratified by life stage and community characteristics. We will conduct 30 focus groups across three urban sites (10 per site) with approximately 210 participants total. Groups will be segmented by: (1) pregnancy trimester (first/second, third), (2) postpartum status (≤6 months, >6 months), and (3) community health worker engagement (engaged, non-engaged). This segmentation allows exploration of communication needs across maternal lifecourse stages and health system touchpoints.

### Recruitment
**Eligibility:** Women aged 18+, pregnant or postpartum (≤2 years), resident in low-SES urban neighborhoods (≤200% federal poverty line), English or Spanish-speaking.

**Recruitment strategy:** Community health workers, obstetric clinics, WIC programs, and community partner organizations will recruit participants. We will offer $50 incentives, childcare, and flexible scheduling to maximize engagement. Recruitment will emphasize diversity across race/ethnicity, parity, and healthcare access patterns.

### Data Collection
**Focus group moderator guide** will explore: (1) trusted sources of maternal health information; (2) barriers to accessing and understanding communications; (3) preferred message formats and channels; (4) social networks for health information sharing; (5) cultural and linguistic adaptation needs; (6) recommendations for improving communications.

**Procedures:** Groups will be conducted in-person at community settings or clinics, audio-recorded with consent, and professionally transcribed. Sessions will last 90 minutes. Moderators will use semi-structured protocols to ensure consistency while permitting emergent themes to surface. Demographic data will be collected via brief participant surveys.

### Analysis Plan
**Framework:** Thematic analysis using an iterative, inductive approach combined with deductive coding structured by COM-B model (Capability, Opportunity, Motivation) to organize barriers and facilitators.

**Process:** Two independent coders will develop initial codebook from first five transcripts, apply it to full corpus, and refine through consensus meetings. We will use ATLAS.ti software for data management. Analysis will identify frequency and salience of themes, variations across demographic/situational subgroups, and saturation of codes. Results will be synthesized into actionable communication recommendations organized by channel, audience, and lifecycle stage.

### IRB Considerations
Study will be submitted to [Institution] IRB for review and approval prior to enrollment. Informed consent will be documented with emphasis on risks of group discussion (loss of confidentiality). Participants will receive confidentiality agreements. We will use data security protocols including encrypted storage, restricted access, and secure deletion post-analysis. No audio will be retained beyond transcription; transcripts will be de-identified. Given sensitive topics (maternal loss, trauma), moderators will be trained in trauma-informed facilitation and have referral resources available.

### Timeline
**Months 1–2:** Protocol development, stakeholder engagement, and IRB submission.
**Months 3–4:** Moderator training, recruitment launch.
**Months 5–9:** Focus group data collection (concurrent across three sites).
**Months 10–12:** Transcription, preliminary coding, and thematic analysis.
**Months 13–15:** Synthesis, memo-writing, and refinement; stakeholder feedback sessions.
**Months 16–18:** Manuscript preparation and dissemination.
