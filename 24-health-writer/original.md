# Childhood Obesity in America

## What is Obesity?

Obesity means having too much body fat. Doctors use a measure called BMI to check if kids are a healthy weight. BMI compares height and weight.

## How Many Kids Have Obesity?

About 1 in 5 children in America has obesity. This is about 20% of all kids between 2 and 19 years old. That equals roughly 15 million children.

The number of obese kids has grown a lot since the 1970s. Back then, only 5% of kids had obesity. Today it is four times higher.

## Who is Most Affected?

Obesity rates are not the same for all groups. The rates are highest in Hispanic and Black children. About 26% of Hispanic kids have obesity. About 24% of Black kids have obesity. About 17% of white kids have obesity.

Boys and girls have similar obesity rates, both around 20%.

## Why Does This Matter?

Kids with obesity face health problems now and later in life. They can get type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, and heart disease. These diseases used to only happen in adults.

Kids with obesity may also feel sad or have low confidence. They might face bullying.

## What Can Help?

Families can help by eating healthy foods and being active. Schools can offer healthy lunch choices. Communities can make safe places to play and exercise.

Doctors can help families make healthy choices for their kids.

## Key Numbers

- 20% of U.S. children have obesity
- 15 million children affected
- 26% of Hispanic children have obesity
- 24% of Black children have obesity
- 4x increase since 1970s
