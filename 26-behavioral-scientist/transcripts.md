# Focus Group Transcripts: Vaccine Confidence Study

## Excerpt 1
"I've read conflicting information online about mRNA vaccines. One source says they're safe, another claims they change your DNA. I don't know who to trust anymore. My doctor said it's fine, but I saw a video from someone claiming doctors are being paid to promote vaccines. It's confusing because I want to protect my family, but I'm genuinely worried about long-term effects we might not know about yet. My sister got vaccinated and had a fever for two days, which made me more hesitant."

## Excerpt 2
"Our community's always been skeptical of government health programs. We remember the Tuskegee study, and frankly, it's hard to forget that. I got vaccinated myself because my job required it, but I understand why many in my church are cautious. We talk about it during meetings, and people share concerns about whether our community's health needs are actually being heard. Some folks got sick after vaccination and blamed it on the shot, whether or not it was related."

## Excerpt 3
"My kids' pediatrician recommended the vaccine, and I trust her completely. She answered all my questions and let me take time to decide. I still had concerns, but her transparency made a difference. I looked up the clinical trial data she mentioned, and that helped. I vaccinated my children because I felt informed and supported, not pressured. Now my family's more confident in vaccines overall because we felt respected during the process."

## Excerpt 4
"Social media is where I get most health info, honestly. My friend group shares articles constantly—some pro-vaccine, some very anti. The anti-vaccine posts get more engagement and seem more emotionally compelling. I haven't gotten boosted because I haven't seen clear information about why I need it. A lot of people I know just skip it. When something goes viral claiming vaccines cause problems, it spreads faster than corrections do."

## Excerpt 5
"I'm a nurse, so I understand vaccine safety data pretty well. I got vaccinated early and encourage my patients to do the same. But I see hesitation from people who distrust pharmaceutical companies or government. Some patients ask me if I'm being forced to promote vaccines. It's frustrating because the data is clear to me, but I recognize many people have legitimate historical reasons for skepticism. I try to listen first before explaining the science."

## Excerpt 6
"Our family decided to wait and see how vaccines worked out for others before deciding. My cousin had some side effects, which confirmed our wait-and-see approach. We looked at statistics but felt like we weren't getting the full picture from official sources. Eventually we got vaccinated because my parents needed protection and we wanted to visit them safely. But we still feel like we were rushed into it and didn't have enough time to research properly."
