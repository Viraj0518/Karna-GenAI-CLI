# KAP Codebook: Vaccine Confidence Study

## KNOWLEDGE (K)
Understanding and awareness of vaccine-related information

### K1: Scientific Literacy
Participant demonstrates understanding of vaccine mechanisms, clinical evidence, or trial data. Includes references to understanding mRNA technology, trial phases, or data interpretation.
- *Example*: "I looked up the clinical trial data" / "understand vaccine safety data"

### K2: Information Exposure
Participant mentions sources where they encountered vaccine information (media, peers, providers, social platforms). Includes passive exposure and active seeking.
- *Example*: "I've read conflicting information online" / "social media is where I get most health info"

### K3: Knowledge Gaps
Participant expresses uncertainty, confusion, or missing information about vaccines. Includes explicit acknowledgment of not knowing or conflicting information.
- *Example*: "I don't know who to trust" / "I feel like we weren't getting the full picture"

---

## ATTITUDES (A)
Beliefs, emotions, and dispositions toward vaccines

### A1: Trust/Distrust in Institutions
Participant expresses confidence or skepticism toward government, pharma, healthcare providers, or medical systems. Includes historical context (Tuskegee, etc.).
- *Example*: "I trust her completely" / "hard to forget Tuskegee" / "distrust pharmaceutical companies"

### A2: Risk Perception
Participant evaluates personal or family-level risk from vaccine side effects, disease, or unknown consequences. Includes worry about long-term effects or specific adverse events.
- *Example*: "worried about long-term effects" / "cousin had some side effects" / "fever for two days"

### A3: Emotional Drivers
Participant expresses emotions (fear, frustration, reassurance, confidence) influencing vaccine attitudes. Includes emotional language and tone.
- *Example*: "confusing" / "frustrating" / "felt informed and supported" / "compelling" posts

---

## PRACTICES (P)
Behaviors, decisions, and actions related to vaccination

### P1: Vaccination Decision
Participant describes whether they got vaccinated, when, for whom, and decision reasoning (required, family, protection, etc.).
- *Example*: "I got vaccinated myself because my job required it" / "vaccinated my children"

### P2: Deliberation/Wait-and-See
Participant describes taking time to decide, seeking information before acting, or delaying vaccination. Includes decision-making processes.
- *Example*: "let me take time to decide" / "decided to wait and see" / "didn't have enough time to research"

### P3: Social Influence/Peer Sharing
Participant describes discussing vaccines with others, peer influence, community norms, or sharing information in social groups.
- *Example*: "my sister got vaccinated" / "friend group shares articles" / "people share concerns during meetings"
